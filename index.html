ABOUT ME

Welcome to my website! My name is Jeromy Lui. I'm currently a junior at UC Berkeley, majoring in computer science and applied mathematics. I'm most interested in doing backend development and am always looking for opportunities to apply my skills. My reason for doing backend is simple - when I see a lamborghini aventador, though the asthetics are brilliant, I admire the engine and the other innerworkings of the car much more.

Out of the classes I've taken at college so far, I enjoy networking(cs168) and real analysis(math104) the most, for various reasons. Ever since I was a child, I always wondered how the internet worked and how devices communicated one another. I was simply awed by the intricacies of internet architecture. Luckily for me, my networking course taught me all of these things, and much more. From overarching ideas such as the different layers of the internet and the end-to-end principle to the specifics such as poisoned reverse for learning switches and the exact structure of a packet in IPv4 and TCP, I've been enlightened and intellectually satifisfied throughly. As for the real analysis course, I had immense satisfaction from solving every challenging problem, whether it was about cauchy sequences or metric spaces. I also learned to adopt a thorough and attentive mindset while working on these problems. Whenever writing proofs, I always had to confirm that every single part logically follows. I had to give attention-to-detail for every single proof I wrote for this class. This gives me an even greater edge against detailed programming projects, where every single little detail matters and one mistake can cause headache-inducing bugs.

One of my main hobbies is to play the piano. Though I used to take lessons in elementary school, I quit when I became a middleschooler because I took it for granted. This is a decision I still sometimes regret. However, I have recently begun to rebuild my skills and play again because many piano pieces resonate with me on a deeper level and I appreciate them. Whenever I play the piano, I let my interpretation take its own course. For me, playing piano is something I do for my own relaxation and enjoyment, not to perform or show to others. That way, I make the music my own, and it will sound the best to me. I am currently learning to play Ballade No.1, Op.23 by Chopin, which I consider a masterpiece. If you aren't familiar with this piece, I highly recommend listening to it - it's amazing.
